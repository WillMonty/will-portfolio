---
title: Staff engineering — what the title actually means
date: 2026-04-24
summary: Lessons from stepping into a staff role and what changed (and what did not).
---

The title change is easy to under-estimate. It sounds like a promotion, and in compensation terms it is. But the job description shifts more than most people expect.

## The work becomes less about code

Not zero code — I still write code every week. But the highest-leverage work is now: spotting the problem before it becomes a project, writing the doc that prevents six different teams from solving the same thing six different ways, and asking the question in the design review that saves three months of rework.

> The best staff engineers I've worked with spend 40% of their time on things that never show up in a sprint.

## Influence without authority

You rarely have direct authority over the people doing the work. You have to convince, not instruct. That means your technical credibility is a resource — spend it on things that matter, protect it by being right more often than not, and never use it to win arguments that don't need winning.

## What did not change

The craft still matters. You cannot influence engineers who do not respect your technical judgement, and you cannot maintain that judgement without staying close to the code. The title does not let you stop caring about the details — it raises the bar on which details deserve your attention.

## The honest summary

Staff engineering is a different job than senior engineering, not a bigger version of it. If you love shipping features and seeing the direct result of your code in production, senior is a great place to stay. If you want to shape how a whole team or organization builds things, staff is worth pursuing — with eyes open to what you are trading away.
